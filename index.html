How it works

Four steps from training to earning.

1

Train

Run CMA-ES on your GPU to evolve a routing head. python -m trinity.train --benchmark math500

2

Pack

Extract your head weights + SVF scales. python scripts/pack_submission.py --run-dir ... --miner-name you

3

Submit

Open a PR with your head_weights.npy + receipt. One submission per week.

4

Earn

If your accuracy beats the current best, your PR is merged and you earn TAO via Gittensor.

FAQ

What does it cost to compete?

~$25-65 in Fireworks API credits per training run. You also need a GPU (T4 ~$0.20/hr) or CPU (free, slower).

How is my head evaluated?

Your head is tested against 200 hidden questions with pre-cached model answers (70% weight), plus 20 live multi-turn questions with real API calls (15% weight), plus efficiency and novelty scores.

Can I see the hidden benchmark?

No. The benchmark is encrypted and stored outside the repo. It is never revealed to miners — this prevents overfitting and keeps the competition fair.

What if I lose?

Your PR is closed with your composite score and the current best shown. You do NOT get component breakdowns (hidden accuracy, live accuracy, etc.) — this prevents benchmark probing. You can retrain and submit again next week.

Can anyone cheat?

Every submission passes 8 automated anti-cheat gates before evaluation: rate limiting, weight validation, duplicate detection (cosine similarity), receipt cross-validation, and more. Fabricated receipts and copied heads are rejected automatically.

How does Gittensor pay me?

When your routing head PR is merged, Gittensor validators (Subnet 74 on Bittensor) detect the merged PR and allocate TAO rewards proportional to your contribution.
